The Unbreakable Vow Theory

By Taurian_Thyme & CrimsonWolf2


Ok I think everyone knows concept of "guilty unless proven innocent".

Basically the meaning of this statement is unless you prove you are innocent, you are guilty.

My theory is about Unbreakable Vows.

How this statement can actually apply to an Unbreakable Vow?

An Unbreakable Vow is a promise that you cannot break - if you do, you die.

Think about it: if your vow was that you could not die, then this sentence would be a very simple way to play an unbreakable vow.

Unless you die you haven't broken it, and when you do, what can the vow do to you?

Kill a dead soul?

So what would actually happen if your Unbreakable Vow is that you can't die?

I really want Rowling to give us an answer to this.
